"Street Studio", a participatory photography project by Alexia Webster

How winning the 2013 Artraker Award changed Alexia Webster's life

  • "The award has been transformative to my work as it afforded me the support to help me take my Street Studio project to a IDP (Internally Displaced Persons) camp in the Eastern DR Congo where I photographed and printed family portraits for over 800 people in the camp who had fled their homes and lives to escape violent fighting over a year before."

  • "The Street Studio project which Artraker first supported in 2013 has since traveled to five countries around the world. This year we will be embarking on a traveling global studio that can provide family portraits and a music and storytelling archive for displaced people in camps around the world."

  • "Artraker is an invaluable asset to artists working in this field. A lot of the work we do in conflict areas is not for profit and therefore there is a dire need to have this work funded and supported and very importantly acknowledged."
